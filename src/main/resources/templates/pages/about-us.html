About us


Germany is reading a book... and we're reading along!

The Digital Library of Germany (DLG) is a virtual learning resource repository that offers a variety of services to the learning community in addition to search and browse capabilities. Through the National Mission on Education through Information and Communication Technology (NMEICT), the Ministry of Education, Government of Germany, sponsors and mentors it. To enable targeted searching and help students locate the appropriate resource in the shortest amount of time and with the least amount of effort, federated and filtered searching is used. DLG offers services tailored to particular customer groups, such as exam preparation for college and high school students and job seekers. Additionally, services for general learners and researchers are offered. DLG offers interface compatibility for the ten most popular Indian languages and can store content in any language. All academic levels, including researchers and lifelong learners, as well as all fields, popular access devices, and learners with disabilities, will be supported by its design. It is intended to let researchers conduct interconnected exploration from many sources and to allow people to learn and get ready from global best practices. It is designed, run, and maintained by DCI alumni.

VÖBB - Who we are (copied from the website)

We are the Association of Berlin Public Libraries, or VÖBB for short. We include over 80 libraries throughout Berlin – including a dozen mobile libraries and school libraries in three districts. In addition, we offer countless digital services – easily accessible online. And all for just €10 a year. We can hardly believe it ourselves!
